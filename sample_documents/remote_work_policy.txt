TechNova Solutions Remote Work Policy
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Document Owner: Human Resources Department

1. PURPOSE

This policy defines the rules and expectations for employees who work
remotely, whether on a hybrid schedule or fully remote basis.

2. ELIGIBILITY

Eligible employees may work remotely up to two days per week with manager
approval. Employees in roles that require physical presence, such as
facilities, on-site IT support, or lab-based hardware testing, are not
eligible for regular remote work and must coordinate any exceptions directly
with their department head.

Fully remote arrangements (more than two days per week) require written
approval from the employee's manager and the relevant department VP, and are
reviewed every 6 months.

3. INTERNATIONAL REMOTE WORK

Employees may not work from another country without written approval from
both Human Resources and Legal. This includes short-term work from a
different country while traveling. Unapproved international remote work can
create tax, immigration, and data residency risks for the company and the
employee. Requests must be submitted at least 4 weeks in advance using the
International Remote Work Request form on the HR intranet page.

4. HOME OFFICE REQUIREMENTS

Employees working remotely must have:
  - A reliable internet connection with a minimum of 25 Mbps download and 5
    Mbps upload speed
  - A dedicated, reasonably quiet workspace suitable for video calls
  - The company-issued laptop, configured and encrypted by IT, as the only
    device used to access company systems

TechNova provides a one-time home office stipend of 400 dollars for eligible
full-time employees to purchase a desk, chair, or monitor. Requests are
submitted through the Expense system and require manager approval, following
the same receipt rules described in the Travel and Expense Policy.

5. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

Remote employees must connect to company systems using the SecureConnect VPN
client, described in the VPN Setup Guide, whenever accessing internal tools,
shared drives, or customer data. Employees may not use public Wi-Fi networks
without the VPN enabled. Screens must be locked whenever the employee steps
away from their workspace, and confidential material should not be visible
to household members or visible in the background of video calls.

6. CORE HOURS AND AVAILABILITY

Remote employees are expected to be available during the same core
collaboration hours defined in the Employee Handbook: 11:00 AM to 3:00 PM
Eastern Time. Employees should keep their calendar accurate and their status
in the company chat tool up to date to reflect availability.

7. EQUIPMENT AND SUPPORT

IT-issued equipment remains company property and must be returned upon
termination or when an employee transitions to a role that is no longer
remote-eligible. Hardware issues for remote employees are supported by the IT
Help Desk at extension 4400; loaner equipment can be shipped within 2
business days for employees more than 50 miles from a TechNova office.

8. PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS

Remote work arrangements do not change performance expectations. Managers
evaluate remote employees using the same goals and review cycle described in
the Employee Handbook. Consistent unavailability during core hours or missed
deadlines may result in the remote work arrangement being revoked.

9. REVOKING REMOTE WORK ELIGIBILITY

TechNova reserves the right to revoke remote work eligibility for business
needs or performance reasons, with a minimum of 2 weeks' notice except in
cases involving a policy violation, such as unapproved international work or
a security incident, which may result in immediate revocation.

10. QUESTIONS

Direct any questions about this policy to hr@technova.example.com. Security
related questions should be sent to security@technova.example.com.
