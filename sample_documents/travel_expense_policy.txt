TechNova Solutions Travel and Expense Policy
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Document Owner: Finance Department

1. PURPOSE

This policy governs business travel booking and expense reimbursement for
all TechNova Solutions employees. It applies to domestic and international
travel undertaken for company business, including customer visits,
conferences, and internal offsites.

2. TRAVEL BOOKING

All flights and hotels must be booked through the company travel portal
(TravelHub) whenever available, so that TechNova can maintain duty-of-care
visibility during travel. Bookings made outside TravelHub require prior
approval from the employee's manager and may still be reimbursed at the
policy's standard rates.

Trips should be booked at least 7 business days in advance when possible to
secure lower fares. Same-day or next-day travel requires manager approval
noted in the expense report.

3. AIRFARE

Economy class is the standard for all flights under 6 hours. For flights
longer than 6 hours, employees may book Premium Economy. Business class
requires VP-level approval and is generally limited to international flights
longer than 9 hours. Airline loyalty program enrollment is encouraged, but
TechNova does not reimburse the cost of elite status purchases or lounge
memberships.

4. HOTEL

The maximum hotel reimbursement is 250 dollars per night for standard U.S.
cities. For designated high-cost cities (New York, San Francisco, Boston,
Washington D.C., London, and Dublin), the maximum is 350 dollars per night.
Employees should book standard room categories; upgrades to suites or club
level rooms are not reimbursable unless it is the lowest available rate at
the property.

5. MEALS AND INCIDENTALS

Employees may claim up to 75 dollars per day for meals while traveling on
company business, inclusive of tips. Meals provided as part of a conference
or by a customer should not be separately claimed. Alcohol is reimbursable
only when it is a minor part of a client entertainment meal and does not
exceed 20 dollars of the daily total.

6. GROUND TRANSPORTATION

Employees should use the most cost-effective reasonable transportation option
(ride-share, taxi, public transit, or rental car). Rental cars should be
booked at the mid-size class or lower unless transporting equipment or more
than 3 colleagues. Personal vehicle use for business travel is reimbursed at
the current IRS standard mileage rate, published annually on the Finance
intranet page.

7. RECEIPTS AND DOCUMENTATION

Receipts are required for expenses greater than 25 dollars. Expenses at or
below 25 dollars may be claimed without a receipt but must still include a
brief description. Credit card statements alone are not sufficient
documentation; an itemized receipt is required for meals and hotel folios.

8. SUBMITTING EXPENSE REPORTS

Expense reports must be submitted through the Expense system within 14
calendar days of the trip's end date. Reports submitted more than 60 days
after the trip may be denied at Finance's discretion. Each report requires
manager approval before it is routed to Finance for payment, which is
typically issued within 10 business days of final approval.

9. CORPORATE CARD

Employees who travel more than twice per quarter are eligible for a company
corporate card, requested through Finance. Corporate card charges must still
be submitted with a complete expense report; the card is a payment
convenience, not pre-approval of the expense. Personal charges on the
corporate card are not permitted and must be reimbursed to the company
within 5 business days if they occur in error.

10. NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES

The following are not reimbursable under any circumstances:
  - Traffic or parking fines
  - In-flight entertainment or Wi-Fi for personal use
  - Personal travel insurance (TechNova provides coverage automatically for
    booked business trips)
  - Spa services, mini-bar charges, and movie rentals
  - Airline change fees caused by personal schedule changes, unless approved
    in advance by a manager

11. INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL

Employees traveling internationally for business should confirm passport
validity (minimum 6 months remaining) and any visa requirements at least 3
weeks before departure. Combining international business travel with
personal remote work days must follow the approval process described in the
Remote Work Policy.

12. QUESTIONS

Direct any questions about this policy to finance@technova.example.com or
your Finance business partner.
