TechNova Solutions Employee Handbook
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Document Owner: Human Resources Department

1. WELCOME AND COMPANY OVERVIEW

TechNova Solutions is a mid-sized enterprise software company headquartered in
Boston, Massachusetts, with regional offices in Austin, Texas; Denver, Colorado;
and Dublin, Ireland. Founded in 2011, the company builds data infrastructure and
knowledge management tools for enterprise customers across financial services,
healthcare, and manufacturing. TechNova employs approximately 1,400 people
worldwide.

Our mission is to help organizations turn scattered information into reliable,
actionable knowledge. Our core values are:
  - Customer trust before short-term revenue
  - Transparency in decision-making
  - Ownership: employees are empowered to solve problems without waiting for
    permission
  - Continuous learning and constructive feedback

2. EMPLOYMENT CLASSIFICATION

Employees are classified as one of the following:
  - Full-time: scheduled for 40 hours per week, eligible for full benefits
  - Part-time: scheduled for fewer than 30 hours per week, eligible for
    prorated benefits after 90 days of service
  - Contractor: engaged through a statement of work, not eligible for
    company benefits, managed by Procurement rather than HR

3. STANDARD WORKING HOURS

The standard working schedule is Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM in
the employee's local time zone, with a one-hour unpaid lunch break. Core
collaboration hours across all offices are 11:00 AM to 3:00 PM Eastern Time,
during which employees are expected to be reachable for meetings.

Employees who need a flexible schedule outside of core hours must have written
approval from their manager. Non-exempt employees must record all hours worked
in the company timekeeping system (WorkClock) no later than the following
business day.

4. CODE OF CONDUCT

Employees are expected to act with integrity, treat colleagues and customers
with respect, and comply with all applicable laws and company policies.
Harassment, discrimination, and retaliation of any kind are strictly
prohibited. Employees who experience or witness a violation should report it
to their manager, any HR representative, or through the confidential Ethics
Hotline at ethics@technova.example.com.

Conflicts of interest, including outside employment that competes with
TechNova or financial relationships with vendors, must be disclosed to HR in
writing before they arise or as soon as they are identified.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION

Employees must protect confidential company and customer information at all
times, including trade secrets, unreleased product plans, customer data, and
personally identifiable information (PII). Confidential information may not be
shared outside the company, including with family members, without written
authorization from the employee's department head.

All company devices must be encrypted and secured with a passcode or
biometric lock. Confidential documents should never be stored on personal
cloud drives or personal email accounts. Any suspected data breach must be
reported immediately to security@technova.example.com or the IT Help Desk at
extension 4400.

6. EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY

TechNova Solutions is an equal opportunity employer. All employment decisions,
including hiring, promotion, compensation, and termination, are made without
regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability,
veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other status
protected by applicable law.

7. WORKPLACE HEALTH AND SAFETY

Employees working from a TechNova office must follow all posted safety
procedures, including emergency evacuation routes, which are reviewed during
new-hire orientation. Any workplace injury must be reported to a manager and
to HR within 24 hours using the Incident Report form available on the
intranet.

8. PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

Formal performance reviews are conducted twice per year, in June and
December. Reviews are based on goals set collaboratively with the employee's
manager at the start of each review cycle. New employees receive an informal
30/60/90-day check-in in addition to the standard review cycle.

9. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS

TechNova generally follows a progressive discipline process consisting of a
verbal warning, a written warning, a final written warning, and termination.
Serious violations of policy, including safety violations, harassment, theft,
or breach of confidentiality, may result in immediate termination without
following the standard progression.

10. BENEFITS OVERVIEW

Full-time employees are eligible for medical, dental, and vision insurance
beginning on the first day of the month following their start date. TechNova
matches 401(k) contributions up to 4% of base salary. Full details on leave
entitlements are available in the Leave Policy document, and remote work
eligibility is described in the Remote Work Policy document.

11. TERMINATION AND OFFBOARDING

Employees who resign are asked to provide at least two weeks' written notice.
On the final day of employment, employees must return all company equipment,
including laptops, access badges, and any physical documents, to their
manager or IT. Final paychecks, including any accrued and unused vacation
balance, are issued according to state or country law.
